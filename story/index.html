Epic Toronto Tourist Weekend

This itinerary of a 3 day getaway in Toronto, Ontario is guaranteed to give you an outstanding experience of all the hot tourist spots the city has to offer.

Use this itinerary and interactive map to help guide you to the many destinations around the city. Each stop is listed bellow with information about the location and when it is suggested you visit. The map can be used to get your bearings on each stop, and spy other points of interest along the way. Each stop is numbered so you can insure you are headed to the next destination on your trip. To learn more about each stop click on the Trip Stop icon, to deselect click anywhere else on the map. You can zoom in and out within the map to get a better view of the locations!

Day One - Arrival

Stop One: The Anndore House

Arrive at your home base for the weekend - The Anndore House. Long before The Anndore House became one of Toronto's most stylish and celebrated hotels, it was The Anndore Hotel & Apartments, a charming 10-story brick building that opened in the 1950's. It was home to many of Toronto's most eccentric personalities and famous faces.

Stop Two: Hemingway's Restaurant

After you have had the chance to settle into your new digs it's time for your first restaurant of the trip! Famous for their incredible rooftop patio, Hemingway's is an iconic Toronto spot! They have called Yorkville home for 39 years, known as their “home away from home”, being all the way from New Zealand.

Day Two - Cultural Enrichment

Stop Three: ROM - The Royal Ontario Museum

Get ready to start the trip off with a BANG! Make your way to the Royal Ontario Museum, known as the ROM to locals! The Royal Ontario Museum is a museum of art, world culture and natural history in Toronto, Ontario, Canada. It is one of the largest museums in North America and the largest in Canada. It attracts more than one million visitors every year, making the ROM the most-visited museum in Canada

Stop Four: AGO - The Art Gallery Of Ontario

Once you have soaked in all the history of the ROM head over to the Art Gallery of Ontario for some exciting exhibits! The Art Gallery of Ontario is an art museum in Toronto, Ontario, Canada. The museum is located in the Grange Park neighbourhood of downtown Toronto, on Dundas Street West between McCaul and Beverley streets just east of Chinatown

Stop Five: Seven Lives Tacos y Mariscos

Once your ready to start winding down the day make your way over to Seven Lives Tacos y Mariscos to have you fill of tacos! Seven Lives is located in the heart of Toronto's historic Kensington Market. They established ourselves in 2012 with a mission serve the freshest and tastiest Baja style tacos in a fun and exciting atmosphere.

Day Three - The Fun Zone

Stop Six: CN Tower

Even though the trip is almost over there is still some time to squeeze in some fun! Head down to the CN Tower; a signature icon of Toronto's skyline. Teh CN Tower attracts more than two million international visitors annually. It houses several observation decks, a revolving restaurant at some 1,151 feet (351 metres), and an entertainment complex

Stop Seven: Ripley's Aquarium

Next up oh the adventure - Canada's Ripley's Aquarium! Ripley's Aquarium of Canada is a public aquarium in Toronto, Ontario, Canada. The aquarium is one of three aquariums owned-and-operated by Ripley Entertainment. It is located in downtown Toronto, just southeast of the CN Tower

Stop Eight: The Rec Room

The final stop on your weekend getaway? Grab som grub and have sum fun at The Rec Room! Located at Roundhouse Park in downtown Toronto, just steps away from the CN Tower, Rogers Centre, Metro Toronto Convention Centre, Air Canada Centre and Billy Bishop Airport. The Rec Room redefines the meaning of fun with over 40,000 sq. feet of great games, mouth-watering eats and amazing entertainment, all packed under 1 roof!
